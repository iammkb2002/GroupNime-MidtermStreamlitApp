Data Cleaning¶

Based on the initial exploration, we proceed to clean the data to prepare it for analysis.

Handling Missing Values¶

We observed that the 'Arrival Delay in Minutes' column has missing values. Since the missing values are a small fraction of the dataset, we decide to remove these rows.

In [7]:
# Drop rows with missing 'Arrival Delay in Minutes'
df = df.dropna(subset=['Arrival Delay in Minutes'])

Data Type Conversion¶

We convert categorical columns to the 'category' data type for more efficient memory usage and better analysis.

In [8]:
# Convert categorical columns to 'category' data type
categorical_cols = ['satisfaction', 'Gender', 'Customer Type', 'Type of Travel', 'Class']
for col in categorical_cols:
    df[col] = df[col].astype('category')

# Convert 'Arrival Delay in Minutes' to integer type
df['Arrival Delay in Minutes'] = df['Arrival Delay in Minutes'].astype(int)

Check for Duplicates¶

We check for and remove any duplicate rows to ensure data integrity.

In [9]:
# Remove duplicate rows if any
df = df.drop_duplicates()

Outlier Detection and Handling¶

We detect and handle outliers in 'Departure Delay in Minutes' and 'Arrival Delay in Minutes' by capping them at the 99th percentile.

In [10]:
# Cap outliers at the 99th percentile
departure_delay_cap = df['Departure Delay in Minutes'].quantile(0.99)
arrival_delay_cap = df['Arrival Delay in Minutes'].quantile(0.99)

df['Departure Delay in Minutes'] = np.where(
    df['Departure Delay in Minutes'] > departure_delay_cap,
    departure_delay_cap,
    df['Departure Delay in Minutes']
)

df['Arrival Delay in Minutes'] = np.where(
    df['Arrival Delay in Minutes'] > arrival_delay_cap,
    arrival_delay_cap,
    df['Arrival Delay in Minutes']
)

Conclusions and Recommendations¶

Based on our analysis, we have identified key insights and potential areas for improvement for the airline.

Key Insights¶

  • Passenger Demographics: The airline serves a wide age range, with a significant portion of passengers between 27 and 51 years old. Younger passengers tend to take longer flights.
  • Flight Operations: The airline operates a mix of short-haul, medium-haul, and some long-haul flights, as indicated by the bimodal distribution of flight distances.
  • Delays: While most flights depart and arrive on time, significant delays exist and are highly correlated between departure and arrival times.

Recommendations¶

  1. Address Significant Delays:
    • Investigate root causes such as operational inefficiencies, air traffic congestion, or maintenance issues.
    • Implement predictive maintenance and better scheduling to reduce delays.
  2. Enhance On-Time Performance:
    • Focus on improving ground operations to expedite boarding and turnaround times.
    • Communicate proactively with passengers about delays to improve satisfaction.
  3. Customer Satisfaction Initiatives:
    • Develop targeted marketing strategies for different age groups, especially the younger demographic on longer flights.
    • Offer personalized services or loyalty programs to enhance the passenger experience.
  4. Optimize Flight Routes:
    • Analyze profitability of various routes to focus on high-demand, high-margin flights.
    • Adjust flight frequencies based on demand patterns to improve load factors.

Conclusion

By addressing delays, enhancing customer satisfaction, and optimizing operations, the airline can improve overall performance and strengthen its competitive position in the market. Continuous data analysis and monitoring are essential to adapt to changing market conditions and customer needs.
